# Meridian Trade & Logistics — Executive Summary

## Supply Chain Delay Analysis (2022–2024)

### Context

Meridian Trade & Logistics Pte. Ltd. engaged our team to analyze delivery performance across their Southeast Asian and East Asian shipping network. The company's on-time delivery rate had fallen below acceptable levels, and the COO needed a data-driven assessment of root causes and actionable recommendations.

**Data scope:** 8,000 shipments | 15 ports | 13 carriers | Jan 2022 – Jun 2024

---

### Key Findings

#### 1. On-Time Delivery Rate: 45.8%

Less than half of all shipments arrive on schedule. The industry benchmark for regional logistics is 70-80%, putting Meridian significantly below standard.

#### 2. Root Causes

The top three delay causes are port congestion (especially Jakarta, Manila, Ho Chi Minh City), customs clearance issues compounded by documentation errors, and weather disruptions during monsoon season.

#### 3. Monsoon Effect

June through September shows a 5-8 percentage point increase in delay rates for Southeast Asian routes. This is a predictable, recurring pattern that should be factored into scheduling.

#### 4. Sea vs Air

Sea freight delay rate (55.8%) is notably higher than air freight (47.6%). Average delay for sea is 3.7 days vs 3.2 days for air. Time-sensitive cargo should consider mode switching during peak congestion.

#### 5. Carrier Variance

OTD rates range from ~42% to ~52% across carriers. The gap between best and worst performers is approximately 10 percentage points, suggesting room for carrier optimization.

#### 6. COVID Hangover

2022 H1 showed elevated port congestion, a residual effect of pandemic-era supply chain disruptions. Performance improved through 2023 but remains below target.

---

### Recommendations

1. **Carrier SLA Renegotiation** — Set minimum OTD targets (60%) with penalty clauses for underperformers. Shift volume toward top-performing carriers.

2. **Pre-Clearance Protocol** — Submit customs documentation 48 hours before departure. Reduce documentation errors with standardized checklists per destination country.

3. **Monsoon Buffer** — Add 2-3 buffer days to scheduled arrival for SEA routes during Jun-Sep. Communicate adjusted ETAs proactively to clients.

4. **Port Diversification** — Reduce dependency on congested ports (Jakarta Tanjung Priok, Manila). Evaluate alternative ports like Surabaya and Hai Phong.

5. **Mode Switching** — For time-sensitive cargo during peak congestion, offer air freight as an expedited option with transparent cost comparison.

---

### Methodology

- **Data period:** January 2022 – June 2024
- **Delay definition:** actual arrival > scheduled arrival
- **Tools:** Python (pandas, seaborn, matplotlib), SQL, Power BI
- **KPI framework:** OTD rate, average delay, transit time, cost per shipment
